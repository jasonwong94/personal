Skule Life + Extra Curriculars

Involved with Spark (Design Club), UTRA and part of the Undergraduate Research Day Commitee (UnERD)

As an engineering student, I try to balance out between my social and academic life. As a student interested who enjoys creating and designing products and even playing sports, I like to join clubs that involve my creativity and thinking skills. As the courses I take in upper years become more interesting, I hope that I will be able to contribute more towards the clubs.

UnERD

Logistics Director

This annual event is for undergraduate engineering research summer students to showcase their work. Students presented their work either as a presentation (held in lecture rooms) or as a poster (presented at the atrium). Working alongside with other students in the commitee, I was in charge of dealing with scheduling and resource management for the day, which included: room assignments for judges and participants, posterboard layout and catering.

In addition to my logistical responsibility, I also contributed in creating the front cover of the booklet as well as creating posters to advertise the event.

This year's UnERD 2014 managed to reach out to 100+ participants of different engineering categories and interests. For more information visit unerd.skule.ca

UTRA

RepRap (3D printing) and Combat Vehicle

In RepRap, we designed and built a fume cupboard to contain the fumes produced by the printer. In addtion, we conducted diagnostic checks on the printer to see whether the printer had any issues printing objects. This up coming year, I am hoping to be involved in the construction of a new, multi-colour 3D printer.

In Combat, I created a circuit consisting of LEDs for aesthetic reasons. The circuit involved voltage regulators, resistors and LEDs as components. Several prototypes were done to adjust the LED brightness. This coming year, I hope to improve on the system and utilize arduino to add cooler effects.
